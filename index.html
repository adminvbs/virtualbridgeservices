Our Story

Virtual Bridge Services began during one of the most uncertain times in recent history

In 2020, when the world was forced to slow down, Jena, our founder and CEO, along with her partner Karl, found themselves navigating the challenges of a shifting economy. Traditional work became uncertain, and like many others, they looked for a way forward. That’s when freelancing entered the picture.

What started as a temporary solution turned into something transformational. Working from home unlocked a new level of focus and efficiency. More than that, it showed us what was possible when the right people were connected to the right opportunities, regardless of where they were in the world.

That’s when the idea for Virtual Bridge Services became clear.

In 2025, we launched the company with a single goal: to become a bridge. A bridge that connects businesses to high-performing remote professionals. A bridge that opens doors for skilled individuals looking for meaningful work. And a bridge that helps both sides grow, together.

We’ve seen firsthand what happens when the right connection is made. It changes businesses. It changes careers. It changes lives.

That’s the story we’re proud to tell—and the work we’re here to do.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

For Clients
What is Virtual Bridge Services?
Virtual Bridge Services is a Philippines-based outsourcing company that provides highly skilled virtual assistants and remote professionals to support global businesses. We focus on providing customized staffing solutions tailored to your operations and growth.
What services do you offer?
We offer administrative support, technical assistance, bookkeeping, social media management, content creation, customer service, and more—based on your business needs.
How do you ensure quality and accountability?
We implement a performance-based approach, regular check-ins, and time-tracking tools such as Hubstaff (or your preferred method). All VAs undergo onboarding, training, and are expected to meet client KPIs.
How does billing work?
Clients are invoiced monthly based on the agreed service rate. Proration applies if services begin mid-month. We provide a simple invoice with breakdowns and proof of payment.
Is there a contract or minimum commitment?
Yes, we typically require a 3-month minimum term to ensure a smooth ramp-up and measurable results. Longer engagements benefit from improved consistency and retention.
For Contractors / Virtual Assistants
Am I considered an employee of Virtual Bridge Services?
No, all VAs are hired as independent contractors. You will be working with international clients through our platform while maintaining flexibility and accountability.
How do I get paid?
Payments are made monthly via secure transfer methods like Wise or GCash. Your compensation is agreed upon in advance and subject to applicable Philippine tax deductions (if applicable).
Is time tracking required?
Yes, we require time tracking through tools like Hubstaff or client-preferred alternatives to ensure transparency and performance tracking.
Will I receive training or onboarding?
Yes. We provide an orientation on tools, client expectations, and your responsibilities. Ongoing support and performance coaching are also available.
What if I have issues with the client or tasks?
We encourage open communication. You can escalate concerns to our support team anytime. We help mediate and resolve issues professionally.
Do I need to register as a freelancer or pay taxes?
Yes. As an independent contractor in the Philippines, you are responsible for your tax registration and filings. We can guide you through the process if you're unfamiliar.
